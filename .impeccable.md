## Design Context

### Users
Notion users installing a public Chrome extension, with a mix of casual note-takers and experienced Notion users. They encounter the product while reading or researching and want to save a thought, selection, or source without breaking concentration. The first-run goal is to connect a workspace, choose one destination, and reach a working capture flow in under two minutes.

### Brand Personality
Calm, trustworthy, and immediate. The experience should feel native to Notion's product language: lightweight enough to stay out of the way, but explicit about where a note will land and what access is being granted.

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined, quiet utility with warm tinted neutrals, crisp typography, and restrained motion. Prefer a single clear action and progressive disclosure over dashboards, decorative cards, gradients, glass effects, or developer-facing configuration. Support light and dark browser contexts, reduced motion, keyboard use, and narrow extension surfaces.

`DESIGN.md` is the source of truth for palette, typography, shape, component, and accessibility decisions. Apple Quick Note is an interaction reference for fast invocation only, not a visual reference.

### Design Principles
1. Get users to a successful capture before teaching advanced configuration.
2. Show one primary decision at a time: connect, choose a destination, then capture.
3. Keep product infrastructure invisible; OAuth client and broker configuration are developer concerns.
4. Build trust with explicit workspace identity, permission context, durable drafts, and recoverable errors.
5. Preserve the thought-first capture loop: open, write, save, return.
