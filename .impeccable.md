## Design Context

### Users
Existing Notion users capturing a thought, excerpt, or source while they are still working in another webpage. They need the composer to open immediately, keep the thought in focus, preserve optional page context, and clearly confirm whether the note has reached Notion.

### Brand Personality
Restrained, compact, and quietly confident. The interface should feel familiar to a Notion user, calm under routine use, and explicit when delivery or recovery needs attention.

### Aesthetic Direction
Faithful to Notion's current product UI: warm neutral surfaces, dense but readable menus, direct hierarchy, small-radius controls, subtle borders and elevation, and locally bundled NotionInter typography. Light and dark modes are both first-class. Apple Quick Note is an interaction reference only, not a styling reference. Avoid decorative gradients, glass effects, oversized radii, novelty iconography, and generic card-heavy SaaS styling.

### Design Principles
1. Put the thought first: the editor and its current selection take priority over surrounding chrome.
2. Match Notion through proportion and behavior, not decoration: typography, density, icon scale, hover states, grouping, and keyboard navigation should feel native.
3. Keep context optional and visible without adding modal friction.
4. Communicate delivery state with quiet confidence and pair every semantic color with text or an icon.
5. Preserve accessibility and resilience across keyboard use, narrow widths, dark mode, and reduced motion.
