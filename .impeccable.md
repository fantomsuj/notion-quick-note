## Design Context

### Users
Notion users, from casual note-takers to experienced workspace users, capturing a thought, excerpt, or source while working on another webpage. They need the composer to open immediately, keep the thought in focus, preserve optional page context, and clearly confirm whether the note has reached Notion. The first-run goal is to connect a workspace, choose one destination, and reach a working capture flow in under two minutes.

### Brand Personality
Calm, restrained, compact, and quietly confident. The interface should feel familiar to a Notion user, stay out of the way during routine capture, and be explicit about the destination, granted access, delivery state, and recovery needs.

### Aesthetic Direction
Faithful to Notion's current product UI: warm neutral surfaces, crisp locally bundled NotionInter typography, dense but readable menus, direct hierarchy, small-radius controls, subtle borders and elevation, and restrained motion. Prefer one clear action and progressive disclosure over dashboards, decorative cards, gradients, glass effects, oversized radii, novelty iconography, generic SaaS styling, or developer-facing configuration. Light and dark modes are first-class; support reduced motion, keyboard use, and narrow extension surfaces.

`DESIGN.md` is the source of truth for palette, typography, shape, component, and accessibility decisions. Apple Quick Note is an interaction reference for fast invocation only, not a visual reference.

### Design Principles
1. Put the thought first: the editor and its current selection take priority over surrounding chrome.
2. Get users to a successful capture before teaching advanced configuration; show one primary decision at a time.
3. Match Notion through proportion and behavior, not decoration: typography, density, icon scale, hover states, grouping, and keyboard navigation should feel native.
4. Keep context optional and visible without adding modal friction, and keep OAuth and broker infrastructure out of the capture experience.
5. Build trust with explicit workspace identity, permission context, durable drafts, recoverable errors, and clear delivery state; pair every semantic color with text or an icon.
6. Preserve accessibility and resilience across keyboard use, narrow widths, dark mode, and reduced motion.
7. Protect the thought-first loop: open, write, save, return.
